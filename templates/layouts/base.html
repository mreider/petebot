I built Petebot in honor of my father, Pete, who died in 2015 of non hodgkins lymphoma. Pete was a lifelong runner, ran cross country at Harvard University, and held a 1957 record for both the mile (time 4:11) and 2 mile (time 9:21).

Getting Started

To get started with Petebot, you must authorize your account in two places: Strava, and Slack. Here are the steps to get things running:

  1. Connect to Strava using the orange button at the top of this page
  2. Authorize the Strava App
  3. Return to pete.fit, connect and authorize for Slack
  4. Open Slack
  5. You should see Petebot listed as an app
  6. Mention Petebot in a channel where you want updates
  7. Click Invite
  8. Petebot will join your channel
  9. Type @petebot help for instructions


Watching clubs

You must have your Strava club ID to begin watching a club in Slack. You can find the club ID in the Strava URL of the club. For example: strava.com/clubs/231407 - the ID is 231407.

Modifying your activity message

If you would like to create a custom message for your activity updates, you can use the @petebot MESSAGE command. These messages can reference any of the fields in the activity summary at http://strava.github.io/api/v3/activities/#summary. Here are some examples:

Show your name, activity, distance, and avg. speed in meters:
@petebot message woah {athlete.firstname} just went for a {name} and went {distance} at a pace of {average_speed} per meter.

You can also add "_feet" or "_miles" to some fields to convert them as follows:
@petebot message woah {athlete.firstname} just went for a {name} and went {distance_miles} miles at a pace of {average_speed_miles} per mile.
